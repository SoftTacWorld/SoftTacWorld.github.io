Challenge Overview

Can your policy achieve not just task success, but safe and contact-aware manipulation?

SoftTacWorld evaluates robot manipulation policies beyond simple visual task completion. We measure whether the contact process is valid and safety-aware under tactile-grounded criteria.

Unlike existing benchmarks that only check final goal states, our hierarchical metrics decompose success into three nested levels — ensuring that a "successful" grasp is also contact-valid and force-safe.

safe_success ⊆ contact_success ⊆ goal_success

Data Format

Each episode contains synchronized RGB images, tactile sensor readings (GelSight/DIGIT), and proprioceptive state. Data is stored in HDF5 format with consistent observation keys across all tasks.

Rules & Evaluation

Hierarchical metrics that go beyond binary task success.

Evaluation Metrics

MetricDescription
goal_success Task goal achieved and scene state valid
contact_success + tactile contact detected and valid throughout
safe_success + compression within threshold, no slip or drop

Contact & FAQ

Can I use external training data?

Yes, but you must declare all external data sources in your submission. Pre-trained visual encoders (e.g., ImageNet, CLIP) are allowed.

What tactile sensors are supported?

The dataset includes GelSight and DIGIT sensor data. Methods are evaluated on both sensor types. You may also propose sim-to-real transfer approaches.

Can I participate as an individual?

Yes. Teams of any size (including 1) are welcome. Each participant may only be on one team.

How is the final ranking determined?

Teams are ranked by safe_success averaged across all tasks. In case of ties, contact_success is used as the tiebreaker.

Will there be awards?

Yes. Cash prizes and certificates will be awarded to top-3 teams. Details will be announced closer to the deadline.
